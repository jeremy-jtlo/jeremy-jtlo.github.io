How It Started

UPDATE: Due to some disagreements with my client's organization, the DCA 2015 website had to be taken offline until further notice. Apologies for the inconvenience.

The DCA 2015 website is actually the very first freelance task I've agreed to. It was quite unexpected, as I had just started a new semester and was approached by an old friend with a project that "had to be delivered by Friday". Not one to turn down someone I've known since elementary school, I agreed and buckled down. Little did I know what difficulties such time constraints can place on work like this.

Game Plan

With the incredible time crunch, everything from layout and number of pages to styling and alignment had to be cranked out within 6 sessions. With this in mind, I opted to use Foundation and SASS, as I didn't have the time to properly implement a grid system or CMS myself. Thankfully, my contact for this client happened to be a photographer and told me to just focus on the structure of the pages, as he would provide the photos and raw text.

Over the course of 6 work sessions in a small coffee shop along North Road (Coffee Monster; try their affogato!), we built and deployed the site thankfully on time.

Lessons Learned

Debate Camp America's website ended up being quite the test of endurance. Going into it, I was not quite prepared for the gauntlet that awaited me on the other side: writing out pages with non-finalized architecture, filling out schedules for events that hadn't yet been planned, and even implementing features that were never included in the initial spec. The entire experience has shed a new light on how I view freelance work, giving me a newfound appreciation for the structure and stability of my co-op placements.
