How It Started

A good mix of a desire for challenge and a distaste for the "New Tab" pages offered by major browsers like Mozilla Firefox and Google Chrome. As a bit of a picky user, I don't particularly like it when a browser tries to tell me what it is I like and what pages I should be visiting. This project, now dubbed Home Sweet Homepage, was started to remedy the issue.

Game Plan

In the early stages of this project, admittedly the desired features were limited: have the links that the user cares about, time-sensitive greetings, a working clock, and incorporate a search bar. I restricted my toolkit to only include what was offered by the Zurb Foundation framework, and found quite a few enjoyable utilities within it. Topbars, easy grids, and simple CSS extension made the process quite enjoyable.

Lessons Learned

As more people took interest in this project, the more I started to consider how the architecture and initial plans didn't exactly reflect an environment that supports collaboration. Everything was written and understood by me, so it was fine. Having colleagues to talk to for feature requests, issues, and milestone planning really changes my expectations not just for this project, but for myself. With the addition of new team members, HSH now has a dark theme, hover states, and more hands on deck for the next set of features. It's no longer just about me and my needs, now it's about "us".
